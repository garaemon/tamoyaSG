HOME > the Home of Udon "Sanuki"

According to the Ministry of Internal affairs and communications in 2010, Takamatsu city in Kagawa was #1 in Udon consumption, and also in flour for Udon purposes. These values express the love that the Udon people have for their noodles. In terms of the number of stores, it is said that there are more Udon stores than telephone poles. From times past, Udon has been an integral part of their culture and life.

On the Lunar Calendar, July 2nd signified the middle of summer. To relieve the aches and pains from a busy harvest and planting season, Kagawa citizens traditionally eat udon, and this tradition was institutionalized when July 2nd officially became Udon Day. In recent years, Sanuki Udon Festivals have taken place, where many downtown communities get many free udon services.

For every coming year, the "Toshiake Udon" wishes upon thick, long and happiness. Umeboshi, shrimp, minced fish, and carrots will accommodate to your favorite red topping, and the white noodles will contribute to the a very red and white new years. In some shrines, they serve free Udon for beginning of the year greetings. Naturally, the year starts with Udon for these Udon people.

"Did you know that in Kagawa, there are faucets that run broth through them?" This is an urban legend that was born because in self-served stores, there are tanks filled with broth that come out of a faucet. But Kagawa prefecture decided to make this urban legend a reality. In the Takamatsu Airport Gift Shop, people can observe this faucet that runs broth.
